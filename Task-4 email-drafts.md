# Task 4 – Email Etiquette & Professional Communication

## Email Etiquette Checklist

| # | Rule | Applied? |
|---|------|----------|
| 1 | Use a professional/institutional email address | ✓ Yes |
| 2 | Write a clear and specific subject line | ✓ Yes |
| 3 | Address recipient with proper salutation | ✓ Yes |
| 4 | Introduce yourself in the first line | ✓ Yes |
| 5 | Keep email concise and focused | ✓ Yes |
| 6 | Use formal language — no slang, emojis, abbreviations | ✓ Yes |
| 7 | Proofread for spelling and grammar | ✓ Yes |
| 8 | End with a proper closing | ✓ Yes |
| 9 | Include full name, reg. no., and course in signature | ✓ Yes |
| 10 | Reply within 24–48 hours | ✓ Yes |
| 11 | Use CC/BCC appropriately | ✓ Yes |
| 12 | Mention attachments in the body before attaching | ✓ Yes |

## Sample Email 1 – Leave Request

**Subject:** Request for Leave of Absence – 20th March 2026

Dear Professor [Name],

I am Jay Prajapati, a First Year B.Tech student (CSE AI-ML, Registration No. 25BAI10350). I am writing to inform you that due to a medical emergency in my family, I will be unable to attend classes on 20th March 2026. I have already informed my class coordinator.

I will ensure that I cover all missed topics and submit any pending assignments upon my return. I kindly request you to grant me leave for the said date.

Thanking you for your understanding.

Regards,  
Jay Prajapati  
25BAI10350 | B.Tech CSE [AI-ML] | VIT Bhopal University

I appreciate your time and guidance.

Sincerely,  
Jay Prajapati  
25BAI10350 | B.Tech CSE [AI-ML] | VIT Bhopal University

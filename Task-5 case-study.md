# Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention

## Case Study: The 2016 Bangladesh Bank Cyber Heist

### Background
In February 2016, hackers infiltrated Bangladesh Bank's systems and attempted to steal nearly USD 1 billion from its account at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York. They successfully transferred USD 81 million to accounts in the Philippines — making it one of the largest cyber heists in history.

### How the Attack Occurred
- Attackers installed custom malware on Bangladesh Bank's servers to manipulate transaction records
- They compromised credentials for the SWIFT global financial messaging network
- Fraudulent payment instructions were sent to the Federal Reserve Bank of New York
- The attack was timed over a Bangladesh Friday/US Saturday weekend to delay detection
- A spelling error ("fandation" instead of "foundation") in one instruction triggered suspicion at Deutsche Bank, stopping further transfers

### Impact
- USD 81 million stolen successfully
- Bangladesh Bank Governor resigned
- Global SWIFT network vulnerabilities exposed
- Only USD 15 million recovered; USD 66 million still missing
- Major reforms in global banking cybersecurity protocols followed

### Perpetrators
The attack was attributed to the **Lazarus Group**, a cybercriminal organization widely believed to be state-sponsored by North Korea, based on malware signatures and techniques identified by Symantec and BAE Systems.

## Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

| # | Prevention Measure |
|---|-------------------|
| 1 | Use strong, unique passwords — never reuse passwords across accounts |
| 2 | Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts |
| 3 | Never click suspicious links or email attachments — verify sender first |
| 4 | Keep all software, OS, and antivirus programs updated |
| 5 | Use encrypted connections (HTTPS, VPN) especially on public Wi-Fi |
| 6 | Regularly back up important data to offline or cloud storage |
| 7 | Be cautious about what personal information you share online |
| 8 | Learn to identify phishing emails — check sender address, look for urgency |
| 9 | Report suspicious activity to your bank or cybercrime helpline immediately |
| 10 | In India, report cybercrime at cybercrime.gov.in or call helpline **1930** |

## Indian Cybercrime Laws – Quick Reference

| Law | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| IT Act 2000 | Primary legislation governing cybercrime in India |
| Section 66 | Computer-related offences (hacking, data theft) — Up to 3 years imprisonment |
| Section 66C | Identity theft — Up to 3 years + Rs. 1 lakh fine |
| Section 66D | Cheating by impersonation using computer — Up to 3 years + fine |
| Section 67 | Publishing obscene material online — Up to 5 years imprisonment |
| IT Amendment Act 2008 | Strengthened cybercrime prosecution provisions |

## Reflection
The Bangladesh Bank case shows that even sophisticated institutions with significant resources can fall victim to cyberattacks. Ironically, a simple spelling mistake by the attackers prevented an even greater loss. This underscores that cybersecurity requires constant vigilance, training, and multi-layered defenses — not just technology. As a future AI engineer, I am now motivated to take cybersecurity seriously throughout my career.
